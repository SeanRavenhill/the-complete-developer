# The Complete Developer

## Master the Full Stack with Typescript, React, Next.js, MongoDB, and Docker

### Written by: Martin Krause | Published by: No Starch Press

---

#### Followed and executed by: Sean Ravenhill

---

This repo will provide my version control and history as I work through this
book in the pursuit of learning and comprehending the process, systems, architecture
and technology required in developing a full stack application.

The Frontend, The Middleware and The Backend.

This book covers the **_M.E.R.N_** – _MongoDB (database), Express.js (backend framework), React.js (frontend library), and Node.js (runtime environment)_ Tech Stack. With the caveat that JavaScript and React.js will be expanded upon to make use of TypeScript and Next.js

It is March 17th 2026 as I start this. A time of massive AI hype/noise (or maybe it's not and this exercise is futile in the end), but I got into coding and programming... and I perhaps I'm one of the few people left whom actually enjoyed the hard process of learning this type of skill. The process of using the grey matter between my skull to abstract problems into working solution via code. Rather than prompting an **_Agent_**. Maybe this is _naive_, and maybe social media, VC'S & CEO's of big tech corporations and the teams building these _generative_ products are the visionaries.

It just gets up my nose to read so many blogs and posts where **_"coding was never the skill"_**. If that is the case, then I don't suppose a mathematician mastering basics like arthematic was ever the skill either.

I want to understand the **_why_** and the **_how_**, and I want to be able to do that without outsourcing my thinking to an **_LLM_** model or the latest new and shiny **_AI Assisted IDE_** or **_Agent_**.

Since I shipped my first website builds, which I felt were a type of **_software_**, albeit basic examples, this was a craft and skill that I felt resonated with me. I enjoyed it. I enjoyed the process.

So maybe this approach is **_"Caveman Coding"_** and this exercise is indeed a futile one. I suppose I will have at least exercised my cognitive abilities, read a book, and build something while learning some new skills.

I'm just some guy who in 2021 started a career in tech, was made redundant in 2023 and has since not been able to get back into the industry. This is part of an active
attempt to break back in. A Junior in experience... maybe a Mid level developer in
knowledge and skill at this point. Highly frustrated and at this stage cronically unemployed.
